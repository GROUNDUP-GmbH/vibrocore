# References & Literature

## Primary Sources (Design Basis)

### Sonic Drilling Theory

1. **Šporin, J. & Vukelić, Ž.** (2017).
   *Structural drilling using the high-frequency (sonic) rotary method.*
   RMZ – M&G, Vol. 64, pp. 1–10.
   DOI: 10.1515/rmzmag-2017-0001
   - Rod resonance equation: f = c / 2l
   - Power equation: N = F²·t²·f / 2m
   - Sonic head kinematics (Resodyn Corp. diagram)
   - Forces: 22,000 – 127,000 kg in industrial systems
   - Frequencies: up to 150 Hz

2. **Wang, Y. et al.** (2015).
   *Design and model analysis of the sonic vibration head.*
   Journal of Vibroengineering, Vol. 17(5), pp. 2121–2131.
   https://www.extrica.com/article/15873
   - 3D FEM analysis of dual-eccentric vibration head
   - Lagrangian equations for damped forced vibration
   - Natural frequency analysis
   - Experimental validation

3. **Lucon, P.A.** (2013).
   *Resonance: The Science Behind the Art of Sonic Drilling.*
   Dissertation, Montana State University.
   - Comprehensive theory of resonance-enhanced drilling
   - Soil-rod interaction models

## Related Open-Source Projects

6. **Open-Source Drilling Community** (GitHub)
   https://github.com/Open-Source-Drilling-Community
   - Drillstring models, drilling data analytics
   - Fokus: Öl/Gas-Tiefbohrung, nicht Soil Sampling

7. **ARTS Lab Smart Penetrometer** (GitHub)
   https://github.com/ARTS-Laboratory/Smart-Penetrometer-with-Edge-Computing-and-Intelligent-Embedded-Systems
   - UAV-deployable cone penetrometer
   - Edge computing for in-situ soil characterisation
   - Different approach (impact, not vibration)

8. **NASA USDC** — Ultrasonic/Sonic Driller/Corer
   https://ndeaa.jpl.nasa.gov/nasa-nde/usdc/usdc.htm
   - Piezoelectric ultrasonic actuation (> 20 kHz)
   - Planetary exploration focus
   - Different principle (ultrasonic vs. mechanical eccentric)
